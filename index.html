About

Hi! I'm a PhD student in Computer Science at Columbia University, fortunate to be advised by Professor Steven Feiner in the Computer Graphics and User Interfaces (CGUI) Lab and part of the Columbia HCI group.

As an M.S. student I also worked with Professor Junfeng Yang. Before Columbia, I received a B.S. in Computer Science, Mathematics, and Cognitive Science from Rutgers University.

News

    Research

    My work focuses on proxies and digital twins for interaction techniques in XR, alongside the intersection of XR and AI. Recent threads:

    • Teleoperation and robot task specification — bimanual XR authoring of assembly hierarchies (IEEE VR'26) and relative 6DoF task guidance (ISMAR'25).
    • Proxies and reality-linked digital twins — permanent proxies for bimanual manipulation (IEEE VR'25) and fast Gaussian-splat digitization of real objects (IEEE VR'26).
    • XR systems and visualization — message visualization for ECS architectures (ISMAR'24) and AR/VR for ice-sheet science (IGARSS'23).
    • XR for rock climbing — kinesthetic rehearsal of climbing moves (ISMAR'24).
